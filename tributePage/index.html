Leonhard Euler

(15 April 1707 – 18 September 1783)
Swiss Army Knife Personified

A glimpse on Euler's life.

  • Born, raised, and educated in Switzerland.
  • First worked as a professor in the Imperial Russian Academy of Sciences whilst being a medic in the Russian Navy.
  • Next worked as a professor in the Berlin Academy, where he accomplished much of his life's works and discoveries.
  • Returned to working with the Imperial Russian Academy of Sciences near the end of his life.
  • Best known for his enormous contributions to mathematics and physics.
  • His works and discoveries also greatly influenced astronomy, engineering, and music.
  • He worked on so many topics that he is usually the earliest written reference for most of them.
  • To avoid confusion, many of the discoveries and theorems attributed to Euler were named to the next person who proved them, after Euler.
“Nothing takes place in the world whose meaning is not that of some maximum or minimum.”
- Leonard Euler
